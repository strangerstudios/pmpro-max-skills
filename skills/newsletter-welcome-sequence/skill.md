# Newsletter Welcome Sequence

Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new newsletter subscribers: Warm Welcome → Greatest Hits → Biggest Mistake → Origin Story → Soft Sales Pitch.

## Before Writing

**Check for context profile first:**
If a context profile is available, read it before asking questions. Use that context to understand the user's membership niche, audience, and expertise. Only ask for information not already covered.

## Step 1: Gather Inputs

Collect all fields below. If any are missing, ask before writing.

| # | Field | Notes |
|---|-------|-------|
| 1 | Your name | |
| 2 | Newsletter name | |
| 3 | What type of membership do you run? | e.g. courses, community, paid newsletter, association, podcast, coaching |
| 4 | Target audience | Describe ideal subscriber |
| 5 | Audience pain points | 2-3 main challenges |
| 6 | Audience goals/outcomes | 2-3 desired results |

| 13 | Credentials/results | Key achievements for credibility |
| 14 | Paid membership offer | What you'll soft-pitch in Email 5 |
| 15 | Special action for Email 1 | e.g. "reply with the word 'ready'" |
| 16 | Reward for taking that action | e.g. "a bonus resource or quick win" |

**Style guidelines (apply to all emails):**
- Friendly, conversational — speak to ONE reader, not a crowd
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences) with plenty of white space
- Strategic use of **bold** and bullet points for emphasis
- Clear, compelling CTAs
- 300-500 words per email
- Consistent voice across all 5

---

## Step 2: Write All 5 Emails

Use these frameworks:

### Email 1 — The Warm Welcome
*Goal: First impression, set expectations, spark immediate engagement*
- Open with genuine excitement about them joining
- Confirm what the newsletter is and who it's for
- Set expectations: frequency, what they'll get, your voice/style
- Deliver any promised lead magnet or welcome gift
- Give the special action (e.g., "reply with 'ready'") + reward
- Brief preview of what's coming in the next few emails

### Email 2 — The Greatest Hits
*Goal: Deliver immediate value, demonstrate depth*
- Open by acknowledging they're new and promising a shortcut
- Introduce your 3-5 best resources with short, punchy descriptions
- Frame each as solving a specific problem they care about
- Use bullets for scannability
- CTA: click through to your most important resource

### Email 3 — The Biggest Mistake / Misconception
*Goal: Challenge assumptions, position your expertise*
- Open with the common belief everyone in the niche holds
- Agitate: explain why that belief is wrong or incomplete
- Introduce your counter-framework or approach
- Show what's possible when they think differently
- CTA: reply with their take, or point to related content

### Email 4 — The Origin Story
*Goal: Build personal connection, earn trust*
- Start with a relatable "before" moment from your journey
- Walk through the key turning points (use your origin story bullets)
- Show the transformation — what you learned, what changed
- Connect your story to their current situation
- End with why you started the newsletter: you want this for them too
- CTA: soft — invite a reply or share

### Email 5 — The Soft Sales Pitch
*Goal: Introduce an offer naturally, without pressure*
- Open by referencing the journey so far (call back to previous emails)
- Acknowledge their problem one more time — make it feel seen
- Introduce the paid offer as the natural next step, not a pitch
- Briefly describe what it is, who it's for, and the core benefit
- Keep it low-pressure: "only if it's the right fit for you"
- CTA: link to learn more or apply/buy

---

## Formatting Rules (All Emails)

- Address the reader as "you" throughout — never "you all" or "subscribers"
- **Bold** key phrases and outcomes
- Bullet lists for any group of 3+ items
- Emojis: use sparingly (0-2 per email) for warmth, not decoration
- One primary CTA per email — make it impossible to miss
- Sign off consistently with name + title in every email
