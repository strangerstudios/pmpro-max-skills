# Generate Lead Magnet Names

Generate 10-15 high-converting lead magnet names that feel specific, outcome-driven, and instantly valuable, organized by naming strategy so you can pick the angle that fits best.

---

## Why This Works

A lead magnet name is the single biggest factor in whether someone opts in or scrolls past. Most lead magnet names fail because they're vague ("The Ultimate Guide to Marketing"), generic ("Free Email Templates"), or try to be clever at the expense of clarity ("The Inbox Alchemist"). A good lead magnet name should make sense without explanation, feel like a concrete asset (not a blog post), and clearly signal value. All in 6 words or fewer.

---

## How to Use This Skill

When triggered, ask these 4 questions **before generating any names**:

> Before I generate names, I need to understand your lead magnet:
>
> 1. **Who is this lead magnet for?**
> 2. **What single problem does it solve?**
> 3. **What format is it?** (PDF, Notion template, email course, checklist, spreadsheet, etc.)
> 4. **What does this lead magnet prepare the reader to do next?** (What's the logical next step or paid offer?)

Only generate names after all 4 questions are answered.

---

## Naming Principles

A good lead magnet name should:
- Clearly imply the outcome or transformation
- Feel like a concrete step, not the entire journey
- Sound valuable even without additional explanation
- Be short, clear, and skimmable (6 words or fewer)
- Start with a power word, number, or clear benefit
- Be easy to understand at a glance

**Words to avoid** (unless contextually unavoidable):
- Ultimate
- Complete
- Powerful
- Master
- Guide
- Blueprint

These words have been so overused they no longer signal value. They signal generic.

---

## Output Format

Generate 10-15 names organized into 3 sections:

### 1. Outcome-Driven Names
Names that lead with the result or transformation the reader will achieve.

*Examples of the pattern: "The 5-Minute Morning Routine," "Your First 1,000 Subscribers"*

### 2. Mechanism-Driven Names
Names that lead with the method, system, or framework inside the lead magnet.

*Examples of the pattern: "The Stacking Method," "The 3-2-1 Email Formula"*

### 3. Time-Bound or Specificity-Driven Names
Names that use specific numbers, timeframes, or constraints to create urgency and tangibility.

*Examples of the pattern: "7 Emails That Sold $50K," "The 15-Minute Launch Checklist"*

---

## Quality Check

Before presenting names, silently verify each one passes these 3 tests:

1. **Would this make sense without explanation?** If someone saw this name with zero context, would they understand what they're getting?
2. **Does it feel like an asset, not a blog post?** Would someone save this to their desktop, not just skim it?
3. **Does it clearly signal value?** Is the benefit obvious in under 2 seconds?

If any name fails these tests, regenerate internally before responding.

---

## After Delivering Names

Ask the user if they want:
- **Refinements** — Tweak specific names they like
- **Landing page alignment** — Adjust the name to work as a headline
- **Paid offer alignment** — Tighten the connection between the lead magnet name and their product

---

## Quality Criteria

Every name must:
- **Be 6 words or fewer** — Ruthlessly short
- **Avoid generic phrasing** — No "ultimate guide" energy
- **Signal a specific outcome** — Not a vague promise
- **Feel like something worth downloading** — An asset, not content
- **Work standalone** — No explanation needed
