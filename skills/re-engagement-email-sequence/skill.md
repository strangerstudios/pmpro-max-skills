# 3-Email Re-Engagement Sequence

Win back cold subscribers (30+ days inactive) with a tight 3-email sequence: Greatest Hits → Lead Magnet Bribe → Quick Question.

## Before Writing

**Check for context profile first:**
If a context profile is available, read it before asking questions. Use that context to understand the user's membership niche, audience, and expertise. Only ask for information not already covered.

## Step 1: Gather Inputs

| # | Field | Example |
|---|-------|---------|
| 1 | Niche or topic | online courses, membership community, paid newsletter, association |
| 2 | Audience pain point | managing time, generating leads |
| 3 | Audience goal/desired outcome | growing their business, feeling confident |
| 4 | Best content/resources (3-5) | titles + links (blog posts, videos, guides, etc.) |
| 5 | Bonus lead magnet name | "90-Day Content Calendar Template" |
| 6 | Benefit of the lead magnet | save time planning content |
| 7 | Social proof for the lead magnet | "Downloaded by 500+ people" |
| 8 | CTA link | one link used across all 3 emails (or per-email links) |
| 9 | Sign-off name | your name or business name |

Ask for any missing fields before writing.

---

## Step 2: Write All 3 Emails

### Email 1 — The Greatest Hits
**Purpose:** Re-spark interest with a curated collection of best content
**Psychology:** Give them a reason to click without asking for anything — pure value, zero pressure

Must include:
- An opening that acknowledges the silence without guilt-tripping them
- Framing that positions this as a helpful resource, not a sales pitch
- 3-5 best content pieces with titles, brief descriptions (one line each), and links
- Each resource tied to a pain point or goal they care about
- Low-pressure CTA: "Pick the one that's most useful to you right now"
- No mention of unsubscribing or list removal — that comes later

### Email 2 — The Lead Magnet Bribe
**Purpose:** Offer a high-value bonus resource to incentivize re-engagement
**Psychology:** Give them something new and genuinely useful — makes staying feel worthwhile

Must include:
- A hook that leads with the benefit, not the name of the resource
- Social proof for the lead magnet (downloads, results, testimonials)
- 2-3 bullet points on what they'll get / be able to do with it
- Clear, single CTA to download or access the resource
- A brief, non-threatening note: "Grab this and I'll know you're still interested"
- Keep it short — this email lives or dies on the offer's appeal

### Email 3 — The Quick Question
**Purpose:** Direct ask — do you want to stay or go?
**Psychology:** Respects their inbox, creates urgency, removes fence-sitters cleanly

Must include:
- Ultra-short opening — no preamble, get to the point fast
- One direct question: "Do you still want to hear from me?"
- Two clear options presented plainly:
  - Yes → click here to stay on the list (or confirm interest)
  - No → they'll be removed automatically if they don't click
- A brief, honest reason why you're asking (list hygiene, relevance)
- No guilt, no begging — matter-of-fact and respectful tone
- Firm but friendly deadline or consequence ("If I don't hear from you by [date], I'll remove you")
- Keep it under 150 words — brevity is the point

---

## Formatting Rules (All 3 Emails)

- **Tone:** Warm, human, zero desperation — you're doing them a favor by checking in
- **Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences, plenty of white space
- **Length:** Email 1: 200-350 words / Email 2: 150-250 words / Email 3: 100-150 words
- **Subject lines:** Curiosity or direct question style; avoid spam triggers like "We miss you!!!"
- **CTA:** One per email, clear and specific — no competing links
- **No guilt language:** Never say "you've been ignoring us" or "we haven't heard from you in a while" in a shaming way
- **Bold:** Key resource titles, lead magnet name, the yes/no options in Email 3

---

## Subject Line Guidance

**Email 1 — Greatest Hits**
Aim for curiosity or value signal:
- "My best [niche] resources in one place"
- "In case you missed these…"
- "The [niche] content people keep coming back to"

**Email 2 — Lead Magnet Bribe**
Lead with the benefit:
- "A free [resource] for you"
- "Grab this before I move on"
- "[Specific result] — free for subscribers"

**Email 3 — Quick Question**
Direct and short:
- "Quick question"
- "Should I keep sending this?"
- "Still want to hear from me?"

## Sequence Timing

| Email | Send Trigger | Goal |
|-------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 (enters sequence) | Re-spark interest via value |
| 2 | 3-5 days after Email 1 (no open/click) | Incentivize with new resource |
| 3 | 3-5 days after Email 2 (no open/click) | Force a decision — stay or go |

After Email 3: remove or suppress non-responders. A smaller, engaged list always outperforms a large cold one.
